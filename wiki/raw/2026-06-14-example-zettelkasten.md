# The Zettelkasten Method

The Zettelkasten ("slip-box" in German) is a note-taking system popularized by
the sociologist Niklas Luhmann, who used it to publish more than 70 books and
400 articles over his career. The core unit is a single index card holding one
self-contained idea, written in your own words.

## The three kinds of notes

- **Fleeting notes** — quick, throwaway captures of a passing thought. They are
  a reminder to think, not a permanent record.
- **Literature notes** — what you took from something you read, in your own
  words, with the source attached.
- **Permanent notes** — one idea per note, written as if for a stranger, and
  linked to the other notes it connects to.

## Why linking matters

Luhmann's slip-box was powerful not because it stored notes, but because every
note pointed to other notes. Over time the links — not the folders — became the
structure. Ideas surfaced by following trails rather than by remembering where
something was filed.

> The slip-box is a thinking partner: you write to it, and it surprises you with
> connections you'd forgotten you made.

## Takeaways

- Capture in your own words, never copy-paste.
- One idea per note.
- Link generously; let structure emerge from the links.
