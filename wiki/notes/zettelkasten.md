# Zettelkasten

The **Zettelkasten** ("slip-box") is a note-taking system popularized by
sociologist Niklas Luhmann, who used it to write 70+ books and 400+ articles.
Its unit is a single card holding one idea, in your own words — see
[[atomic-notes]].

## Three kinds of notes

- **Fleeting** — quick captures; a reminder to think, not a record.
- **Literature** — what you took from a source, reworded, with the source kept.
- **Permanent** — one idea per note, written for a stranger, and linked. These
  are what mature into [[evergreen-notes]].

## Structure emerges from links

Luhmann's box worked because every note pointed to others. Over time the
**links — not the folders — became the structure**, and ideas surfaced by
following trails. This link-first idea is the backbone of the modern
[[second-brain]].
