# Evergreen Notes

**Evergreen notes** are written to be developed, revised, and reused across
time — not filed once and forgotten. The term (from Andy Matuschak) describes
what a [[zettelkasten]] permanent note becomes when you keep tending it.

## Principles

- **Atomic** — one idea each, so they stay linkable. See [[atomic-notes]].
- **Concept-oriented** — titled by the idea, not the source or the date, so the
  same note accrues links from everywhere it's relevant.
- **Densely linked** — the act of linking forces you to relate new ideas to old
  ones, which is where understanding actually happens.

## Tending over capturing

The hard part isn't capture, it's **retrieval and reuse** — opening one note
should pull related thinking with it. A note you revisit and revise is alive; a
note you only ever stored is dead weight. This is the difference between a
[[second-brain]] and a digital junk drawer.
